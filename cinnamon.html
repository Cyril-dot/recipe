Ingredient Spotlight: Cinnamon

Overview

Description: Cinnamon is a fragrant spice derived from the bark of the Cinnamomum tree, known for its warm, sweet flavor.

Origin: Cinnamon has been used for thousands of years, originating in Ceylon (now Sri Lanka).

Nutritional Information

Nutrient Amount per 100g
Calories 247
Carbohydrates 81g
Fat 1.2g
Protein 4g

Health Benefits:

  • Rich in antioxidants.
  • May help regulate blood sugar levels.

Culinary Uses

Flavor Profile: Cinnamon has a sweet and warm flavor that enhances both sweet and savory dishes.

Cooking Methods: It can be used in baking, cooking, and beverages.

Common Dishes:

  • Cinnamon rolls
  • Apple pie
  • Spiced chai tea

Storage and Preparation Tips

Storage Guidelines: Store in a cool, dark place in an airtight container.

Preparation Tips: Use freshly ground cinnamon for the best flavor.

Varieties and Substitutes

Types:

  • Ground cinnamon
  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Cassia cinnamon

Substitutes:

Nutmeg or allspice can be used as substitutes.

Recipe Suggestions

  • Cinnamon Roll Casserole
  • Spiced Apple Crisp
  • Cinnamon Hot Chocolate

Fun Facts and Trivia

  • Cinnamon was once more valuable than gold.
  • It is used in traditional medicine in many cultures.
