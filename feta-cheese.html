Ingredient Spotlight: Feta Cheese

Overview

Description: Feta cheese is a crumbly, tangy cheese traditionally made from sheep's milk.

Origin: Originating from Greece, feta has been a staple in Mediterranean cuisine for centuries.

Nutritional Information

Nutrient Amount per 100g
Calories 264
Fat 21g
Carbohydrates 4g
Protein 14g

Health Benefits:

  • Good source of calcium and protein.
  • Contains beneficial probiotics that support gut health.

Culinary Uses

Flavor Profile: Feta has a tangy, salty flavor that adds depth to many dishes.

Cooking Methods: Can be crumbled over salads, baked, or used in savory pastries.

Common Dishes:

  • Greek salad
  • Spanakopita (spinach pie)
  • Feta-stuffed peppers

Storage and Preparation Tips

Storage Guidelines: Keep feta in its brine or in a sealed container in the refrigerator.

Preparation Tips: Rinse if too salty, and crumble just before serving for best texture.

Varieties and Substitutes

Types:

  • Greek feta
  • Bulgarian feta

Substitutes:

Ricotta or goat cheese can be used as substitutes for feta in recipes.

Recipe Suggestions

  • Greek Salad with Feta
  • Feta and Spinach Stuffed Chicken
  • Baked Feta Pasta

Fun Facts and Trivia

  • Feta cheese is protected by a PDO (Protected Designation of Origin) status in the European Union.
  • In ancient Greece, feta was often made in large blocks and stored in olive oil.
